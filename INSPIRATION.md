# NABe Inspiration Research

Date: 2026-03-13 (PDT)
Goal: Identify modern, high-quality references (Japanese-first + top U.S./Bay Area ceramics) before finalizing visual direction.

## Quick Design Direction Chosen for NABe

- **Overall feel:** Kyoto-inspired minimal modern + Bay Area warmth.
- **Visual language:** Generous whitespace, earthy neutrals, restrained serif/sans typography pairing, tactile closeups.
- **UX direction:** Mobile-first, clear nav, lightweight pages, extensible structure for future commerce/journal pages.

## Reference Sites (12)

### Japanese / Japanese-aesthetic priority

1. **Hasami Porcelain** — https://hasami-porcelain.com/
   - Notes: Highly restrained composition; object-first imagery.
   - Takeaway for NABe: Let product silhouettes breathe with minimal visual clutter.

2. **KINTO USA (Japanese brand)** — https://kinto-usa.com/
   - Notes: Airy layout and editorial spacing.
   - Takeaway for NABe: Use white space as a premium signal; keep hierarchy calm.

3. **Asahiyaki (Kyoto)** — https://asahiyaki.com/
   - Notes: Heritage storytelling presented with modern simplicity.
   - Takeaway for NABe: Balance tradition + contemporary copy tone.

4. **Sueki Ceramics** — https://www.suekigama.com/
   - Notes: Neutral palette and craftsmanship-forward product presentation.
   - Takeaway for NABe: Warm, muted palette and tactile photography direction.

5. **AITO Japan** — https://aito.co.jp/
   - Notes: Clean brand framing around tableware categories.
   - Takeaway for NABe: Future category structure can remain clean and scalable.

6. **Tokyo Design Studio (Japanese tableware aesthetic)** — https://tokyodesignstudio.com/
   - Notes: Contemporary Japanese patterns with modern product grid UX.
   - Takeaway for NABe: Modular card system for future collections.

### U.S. ceramics / Bay Area references

7. **Heath Ceramics (Bay Area)** — https://www.heathceramics.com/
   - Notes: Strong premium storytelling and consistent materials palette.
   - Takeaway for NABe: Pair sustainability/craft story with clean commerce pathways.

8. **Year & Day (San Francisco)** — https://yearandday.com/
   - Notes: Lifestyle-first landing flow and soft modern tone.
   - Takeaway for NABe: Home page should feel editorial, not catalog-heavy.

9. **East Fork** — https://www.eastfork.com/
   - Notes: Color-led merchandising balanced by straightforward layout.
   - Takeaway for NABe: Maintain clarity when introducing glaze/color stories.

10. **Jono Pandolfi** — https://www.jonopandolfi.com/
    - Notes: Professional utility with clean typography.
    - Takeaway for NABe: Practical navigation + concise, honest product language.

11. **Haand** — https://haand.us/
    - Notes: Elevated minimal branding with robust e-commerce architecture.
    - Takeaway for NABe: Serif/sans pairing and muted accents work well.

12. **Felt+Fat** — https://www.feltandfat.com/
    - Notes: Distinct personality while preserving layout discipline.
    - Takeaway for NABe: Keep voice warm but avoid visual noise.

## Common Patterns Worth Reusing

- Neutral backgrounds with a single warm accent color
- High-quality closeup product imagery (future photoshoot requirement)
- Serif for headlines + clean sans for interface text
- Short sections with clear spacing rhythm
- Minimal top navigation with room for growth
- Story + product utility balanced on homepage

## What NABe Implemented from This

- Mobile-first layout
- Sticky minimal navigation
- Calm color system (earth + off-white + charcoal)
- Home page with brand story and naming explanation (NABe = initials + claypot)
- Dedicated inspiration page listing references
- Component-style sections/cards for future expansion
